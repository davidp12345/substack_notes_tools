Privacy Policy for Notes Toolkit Extension

Last Updated: September 2025

Quick Summary: This extension helps you transfer content from LinkedIn and X (Twitter) to Substack Notes. We only collect the specific post content you choose to transfer, store it temporarily on your device, and don't send it to any servers except Substack when you use the feature.

1. What Data We Collect

Our extension only collects data when you actively use the "Edit in Notes" feature. Here's exactly what we collect:

Data Type What We Collect When We Collect It Where It's Stored
LinkedIn Post Content Text content, images, and links from LinkedIn posts you click "Edit in Notes" on Only when you click "Edit in Notes" on a LinkedIn post Temporarily in your browser's local storage
X/Twitter Post Content Tweet text, images, and embedded links from posts you click "Edit in Notes" on Only when you click "Edit in Notes" on a Twitter/X post Temporarily in your browser's local storage
Extension Preferences Your toggle settings (LinkedIn ON/OFF, X/Twitter ON/OFF) When you change toggle settings in the extension popup In Chrome's sync storage (syncs across your devices)
Temporary Transfer Data Random tokens and timestamps used to coordinate content transfer During content transfer process Temporarily in your browser's local storage

2. How We Use Your Data

We use the collected data for these specific purposes:

  • Content Transfer: To move your selected social media content to Substack Notes
  • Auto-Population: To automatically fill the Substack Notes composer with your content
  • Clipboard Backup: To copy content to your clipboard as a backup method
  • Feature Control: To remember your LinkedIn/Twitter toggle preferences

3. Data Storage and Retention

Important: All data is stored locally on your device. We don't have servers that collect or store your information.
  • Post Content: Stored temporarily (usually deleted within 1 minute after transfer)
  • Transfer Tokens: Automatically cleaned up after successful transfer
  • User Preferences: Stored permanently until you uninstall the extension
  • Permalink URLs: Stored for up to 60 seconds for LinkedIn post redirects

4. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell, trade, or share your data with third parties.

The only data sharing that occurs is:

  • To Substack: When you use "Edit in Notes", your selected content is sent to Substack.com to populate the Notes composer (this is the core functionality you requested)
  • No Analytics: We don't use Google Analytics or similar tracking services
  • No Advertising: We don't share data with advertising networks
  • No Marketing: We don't use your data for marketing purposes

5. How the Extension Works

When you click "Edit in Notes" on a social media post:

  1. Extension captures the content you selected
  2. Content is temporarily processed for transfer
  3. Substack Notes composer opens with your content
  4. Content is auto-populated in the editor
  5. Content is copied to your clipboard as backup
  6. Temporary data is cleaned up after transfer

6. Permissions We Request

Permission Why We Need It What We Do With It
Access to LinkedIn.com To add "Edit in Notes" buttons and extract post content Only access posts you specifically click on
Access to X.com/Twitter.com To add "Edit in Notes" buttons and extract tweet content Only access tweets you specifically click on
Access to Substack.com To auto-populate the Notes composer Only when you're using the Notes composer
Storage To temporarily store content during transfer Store content locally on your device only
Active Tab To interact with the current webpage Only on LinkedIn, Twitter, and Substack pages

7. Your Privacy Rights

You have complete control over your data:

  • Turn Off Features: Use the toggle switches to disable LinkedIn or Twitter functionality
  • Clear Data: Uninstall the extension to remove all stored data
  • Selective Use: Only posts you click "Edit in Notes" on are processed
  • No Tracking: We don't track your browsing or create user profiles

8. Security

We implement several security measures:

  • All data processing happens locally on your device
  • Content is only sent to Substack when you explicitly use the feature
  • Temporary tokens expire quickly (within minutes)
  • No sensitive authentication data is stored
  • Extension only works on specific, trusted websites

9. Children's Privacy

This extension is not intended for use by children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

10. Changes to This Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the new policy.

11. Open Source

This extension's code can be reviewed for transparency. You can verify exactly what data is collected and how it's used by examining the source code.

12. Contact Information

If you have questions about this privacy policy or how your data is handled:

You can review the extension's source code to verify our privacy practices, or contact us through the Chrome Web Store listing.

Data Controller: The individual or organization who published this extension

Data Processing Location: Your local device (no external servers)

This privacy policy is designed to be transparent and easy to understand. If you have any questions about how your data is handled, please don't hesitate to reach out.
